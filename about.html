👋 About Us

A free tool built to save you a trip to the photo shop.

DocStitcher exists to make ordinary paperwork — ID scans, passport photos, signed forms — quick to prepare from your phone, without paying anyone or handing your documents to a stranger's server.

Why we built this
The paperwork problem

Most of us have stood in line at a local photo studio or cyber café just to get an Aadhaar card scanned front-and-back onto one A4 page, or to have a passport-size photo resized to the exact specs a government portal demands. It's a small task that somehow always costs money and takes longer than it should.

DocStitcher started as a way to do that ourselves, in the browser, for free. It grew from "stitch two photos into an A4 PDF" into a small toolbox: passport/ID photo sizing, background removal, signature placement, cropping and compressing files down to a target size, watermarking, and bulk PDF building — the handful of things people actually need before uploading a document somewhere.

We're honest that this isn't a big company with a research lab. It's a small, ad-supported project. What we can promise is that we built it to solve a real annoyance, and we kept it free because that annoyance shouldn't cost money to fix.

Honest words, not marketing words
What "free" actually means here

There is no premium tier, no paywalled feature, no "free trial," and no account to create. Every tool on this site — the document stitcher, the passport photo maker, the background remover, the compressor, the watermark tool, the bulk PDF builder — works the same for everyone, with no usage limit we impose.

The only thing that keeps the lights on is advertising. We don't sell your data, because we never receive it in the first place — everything runs inside your browser using JavaScript and Canvas APIs, not on a server we operate. The single, clearly optional exception is described below, because we'd rather over-explain it than have you find out by surprise.

The fine print, said plainly
Privacy, honestly

By default, everything you upload — Aadhaar, PAN, passport photos, signatures, whatever — is processed entirely inside your browser and never touches a server we run. The only network calls DocStitcher makes automatically are to load the page itself and to display the ads that keep it free.

The one deliberate exception: the background remover offers an optional, higher-accuracy mode powered by the third-party service remove.bg. It is off unless you manually turn it on and enter your own remove.bg API key. If you do, that specific photo is uploaded to remove.bg's servers for processing — nothing else is sent, and it never happens automatically. The app also asks you to confirm this the first time, before anything is uploaded.

We'd rather tell you this twice than have you assume the app is 100% local when one optional feature isn't. Full details live in our Privacy Policy, and the rules everyone agrees to by using the site are in our Terms of Service.
